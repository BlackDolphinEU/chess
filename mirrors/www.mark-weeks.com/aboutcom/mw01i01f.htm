Chess Informant's Chess Is Chess : The greats and the near greats
The world champions and their challengers

A popular game among chess history enthusiasts is to name the strongest world champion, the weakest, and the strongest player who never became world champion. Let's consider this game in a new way.

Chess Informant has identified the '200 greatest tournaments in history'. These are invitational events not including world championship qualifiers like interzonals and candidate tournaments. The 200 events cover 18406 games contested by 535 different players.

Let's compute some statistics based on the results of the games played in these 200 events. We can limit our statistics to include only those games where a past, present, or future world champion -- not necessarily the reigning titleholder -- played White or Black. Of course, we'll also include games where world champions played each other, like the many Kasparov - Karpov tournament encounters of the late 1980s and early 1990s.

Our 200 tournaments give us 4778 games with a world champion playing either White or Black. First, here's a count of events in which the champions participated, followed by their scores against all opponents.
