From: Regulations for the FIDE Grand Swiss 2021 [PDF]

1.1. Governing Body of the FIDE Chess.com Grand Swiss Tournament (hereinafter referred to as “Grand Swiss”) is the International Chess Federation (FIDE).

1.2. Grand Swiss shall be organised in Douglas, Isle of Man (hereinafter referred to as “IOM”) from 25 October to 8 November 2021.

1.3. Grand Swiss shall be organised by IOM International Chess Limited (“the Organiser”), with sponsorship from the Scheinberg family (“the Sponsor”)

1.4. FIDE Global Strategy Commission (hereinafter referred to as “GSC”) is in charge of preparing Regulations, communicating with the Organiser and the participants, conducting inspections.

1.5. The body responsible for adopting and changing these Regulations is the FIDE Council, upon recommendation by GSC.

1.6. At any time, any circumstance or unforeseen situation not covered in these Regulations shall be referred to the FIDE President for the final decision.

The rules of the competition were based on the event from the previous cycle, the 2019 Grand Swiss.

2. Qualification for Grand Swiss

2.1. Invitations for Grand Swiss are determined according to the following criteria:

A. Average FIDE Rating of the 12 monthly lists starting from 1st July 2020 to 1st June 2021. One hundred (100) players will qualify by their average rating. For the purpose of deciding one hundred qualifiers by rating, the average ELO from the twelve standard FIDE rating lists from 1st July 2020 to 1st June 2021 is used. In case of equality, two decimals are taken into consideration. If the numbers are still equal then the total number of rated games in all 12 standard rating periods shall be decisive: the player with the most number of games qualifies.

B. Women’s World Champion GM Ju Wenjun.

C. Four (4) Continent spots. Each spot is decided by the respective Continental President.

D. Four (4) players nominated by the FIDE President.

2.2. If any of the one hundred (100) players qualified by path A (Article 2.1.) declines to participate, the reserved spot goes to the next non-qualifying player from path A (Article 2.1.). If Women World Champion GM Ju Wenjun declines to participate, her place goes to GM Aleksandra Goryachkina. If GM Aleksandra Goryachkina declines to participate, for all further replacements path A (Article 2.1.) applies for this purpose. The automatic replacements by path A are in force till 50th reserve player, after which players’ replacement and/or playing spots filling shall be subject to GSC decision.

2.3. The Organiser is entitled to include five (5) additional players of their own choice. This means there will be a maximum of 114 players in Grand Swiss.
